02 · Typography

Type

Two typefaces, each with one job. Golden Batch sets the headlines. Hanken Grotesk handles everything you read in paragraphs, buttons, and labels. Both are self-hosted, so they load fast and look the same everywhere.

The one rule for Golden Batch: regular (400) only. Never bold it. Bold crowds the letters and loses the elegance. The token sets font-synthesis: none so the browser cannot fake a bold weight either. When you need emphasis in a heading, use size or color, not weight.

05 · Voice

How we write

Warm, direct, plain. Write the way you would talk to a client across the table. Speak to the reader as you, and be specific instead of vague.

Sounds like us

"You will have your gallery within two weeks, and every photo is yours to print."

Not us

"We leverage a seamless, robust workflow to elevate and unlock your visual story."

Do

  • Talk to the reader: you, your, yours.
  • Be specific. Real numbers, real timelines.
  • Keep sentences short and plain.
  • Sound warm and sure, not salesy.

Don't

  • No em dashes. Use a period or a comma.
  • Avoid elevate, unlock, leverage, seamless, robust.
  • No vague promises you cannot back up.
  • No filler. If a word does no work, cut it.
